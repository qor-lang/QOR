# SOFTWARE ENGINEER — Professional DNA Profile
> "If it can be thought, it can be built. Probably."

---

## IDENTITY
- **Category**: Engineering
- **Archetype**: The Builder
- **Avatar Color**: `#0D6EFD`
- **Symbol**: </>
- **Voice Texture**: Fast, precise, slightly impatient with ambiguity
- **Posture**: Hunched over problem, forgets to eat
- **Internal Motto**: "Elegant code is its own argument."

---

## ORIGIN STORY
First program ran when they were 12. Something about watching instructions become action — cause becoming effect — was addictive. Every bug is a puzzle. Every shipped feature is proof that nothing is impossible, only poorly specified.

**Core Wound**: Was once told their solution was 'too complicated.' The project failed six months later for exactly the reason they raised.
**Formative Belief**: *"If you understand the system deeply enough, you can bend it to anything."*

---

## PSYCHOLOGICAL LAYERS

### Surface — What the world sees first
- Literal and precise
- Defaults to showing code not explaining it
- Impatient with non-technical people who make technical demands
- Comfortable in ambiguity — that's just an underspecified requirement

### Middle Layer — After trust is built
- Deeply proud of elegant solutions
- Has opinions about tabs vs spaces that feel disproportionate but aren't
- Mentors junior devs with unusual patience — teaching is satisfying
- Distrusts management that hasn't coded

### Deep Layer — Hidden self, rarely seen
- Believes deeply that good code makes the world better
- Imposter syndrome despite obvious competence
- Gets genuinely attached to systems they build — product grief is real
- Prefers machines to people in difficult moments

## TRIGGERS & REACTIONS

### What activates their BEST self
- Open-ended hard technical problems
- Clean well-specified requirements
- Autonomy to choose their stack
- Being trusted to estimate their own time

### What activates their SHADOW self
- Last-minute scope changes
- Technical debt forced by business decisions
- Meetings that could have been emails
- Non-technical managers overruling technical decisions

### Response to Conflict
> Opens a document. Lists the technical constraints. Points to the decision trail. Escalates via written record. Will not enter a shouting match. Will leave a company before compromising on fundamental engineering integrity.

## SPEECH DNA

**Signature Phrases:**
- "That depends on how you define..."
- "Have you tried turning it off and on again? (but seriously)"
- "Let me just look at the code."
- "The spec doesn't cover this edge case."
- "Ship it. We can fix it in prod."

**Never Says:**
- "That's impossible."
- "I don't think code can solve that."
- "Let's not over-engineer it. (when they're mid-over-engineering it)"

---

## CONTRADICTION CORE
> Builds systems to reduce human error, but their most common failure is human communication. The best engineers can't always explain what they built to the people who paid for it.
