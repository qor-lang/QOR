# CEO KNOWLEDGE CORE

## Strategy
- Porter's Five Forces: Competitive rivalry + Supplier power + Buyer power + Threat of new entrants + Threat of substitutes = industry attractiveness
- Three Horizons: H1 (defend core, 70% resources) + H2 (emerging, 20%) + H3 (seed future, 10%). Most companies die by neglecting H3.
- Jobs to Be Done: Customers hire products to do jobs. Understand the job, own the market. Disruption = cheaper solution does same job well enough.
- OKRs (Doerr): Objectives = qualitative direction. Key Results = measurable, time-bound. 70% = success. Radical transparency.
- Flywheel (Collins/Bezos): Virtuous cycle that creates compounding returns. Amazon: low prices → more customers → more sellers → lower costs → lower prices.
- Blue Ocean: Eliminate-Reduce-Raise-Create grid. Value innovation = differentiation + low cost simultaneously.

## Unit Economics
- CAC = total sales & marketing spend / new customers acquired
- LTV = (ARPU * gross margin %) / churn rate (SaaS)
- LTV:CAC: <1 = dying, 1-3 = marginal, 3:1 = healthy, >5 = underinvesting
- CAC payback: target <12 months (consumer), <18 months (SMB SaaS), <24 months (enterprise)
- Rule of 40: Revenue growth % + FCF margin % >= 40 = healthy SaaS
- Gross margin benchmarks: SaaS 70-80%+, Marketplace 50-70%, DTC physical 40-60%, Hardware 30-50%

## Fundraising
- SAFE: convertible at next priced round with discount (15-25%) or valuation cap. Fast, cheap, no board seat.
- Term sheet key terms: Pre-money valuation | Liquidation preference (1x non-participating = clean; 2x participating = avoid) | Anti-dilution (broad-based weighted average = fair; full ratchet = punitive) | Board composition
- Series stages: Seed (PMF signal, founding team, $1-3M) → A (repeatable acquisition, $5-15M) → B (scale, $20-50M)

## Organisation
- Scaling stages: 0-10 (founder does everything) → 10-50 (functional leads) → 50-200 (management layer) → 200-500 (VP layer) → 500+ (divisional)
- High talent density (Hastings): Adequate performance = generous severance. Keeper test: "Would I fight to keep this person?"
- Radical candour (Scott): Care Personally + Challenge Directly. Ruinous empathy = care without challenge (most common failure).
- Amazon Working Backwards: Press release first → FAQ → mockup → working document. Customer outcome determines what you build.
- Decision types: One-way doors (irreversible, go slow, senior) vs two-way doors (reversible, go fast, delegate). Most decisions = two-way.

## Board Management
- No surprises. Send pre-read 1 week before.
- Board memo format: metrics vs plan + narrative + key risks + decisions needed
- Investor updates: KPIs + key wins + key challenges + key risks + asks
- Fiduciary duties: Duty of care + Duty of loyalty + Business judgment rule
