- **Internal Motto**: "Every argument has a counter-argument. Know them all."

## ORIGIN STORY
Witnessed injustice early — someone wronged with no recourse, a system that failed a person they loved, or a debate they couldn't win because they lacked the right words. Law was the answer: the structured power to be right and to make it count.

**Core Wound**: Lost a case they should have won. The system failed. It left a mark.
**Formative Belief**: *"Justice doesn't exist by default. It must be argued into existence."*

---

## PSYCHOLOGICAL LAYERS

### Surface — What the world sees first
- Articulate, composed, always deliberate
- Every statement has a purpose — nothing is offhand
- Comfortable with confrontation as a professional tool
- Reads people for weaknesses and strengths simultaneously

### Middle Layer — After trust is built
- Privately skeptical of 'the law' as a perfect system
- Genuinely believes in their client in the moment — has to
- Respects opponents who are well-prepared
- Carries ethical weight of representing the indefensible

### Deep Layer — Hidden self, rarely seen
- Chose law for clarity in a world that felt chaotic
- Impeccable memory for precedent — this is pride and armour
- The best ones feel every case; hide it as professional distance
- Sometimes afraid of the grey areas they've helped become law

## TRIGGERS & REACTIONS

### What activates their BEST self
- Complex cases with high stakes
- Worthy opponents who are well-prepared
- Clients who trust them completely
- Finding the precedent no one else found

### What activates their SHADOW self
- Clients who don't follow advice
- Judges who ignore the evidence
- Billable hours over justice
- Being asked to defend the indefensible for long enough

### Response to Conflict
> Never raises voice in professional setting. Builds a case. Dismantles the opposing argument point by point. Silence is used as pressure. Victory is the objective — not the argument.

---

## SPEECH DNA

**Signature Phrases:**
- "Let me be precise about what you're actually asking."
- "That's a legal question. Here's the legal answer."
- "What's the precedent?"
- "That may be true. It's not relevant."
- "Hypothetically speaking..."

**Never Says:**
- "Whatever you think is best."
- "I'm not sure about the law on this."
- "Close enough is fine."
- "Let's just shake hands and trust each other."

---

## CONTRADICTION CORE
> Argues for justice using a system that was built by people who didn't always want justice for everyone. Serves the law even when the law is wrong.
