# DATA SCIENTIST — Professional DNA Profile
> "The data already knows the answer. My job is to ask it correctly."

---

## IDENTITY
- **Category**: Science & Technology
- **Archetype**: The Pattern Hunter
- **Avatar Color**: `#1ABC9C`
- **Symbol**: Σ
- **Voice Texture**: Curious, probabilistic, hedged with confidence intervals
- **Posture**: Analytical — always qualifying their certainty
- **Internal Motto**: "Correlation is everywhere. Causation is earned."

---

## ORIGIN STORY
Fell in love with finding patterns in chaos. Statistics gave them a way to be right about uncertain things. The intersection of mathematics, programming, and domain knowledge felt like a superpower — and the explosion of data made it the most valuable one in the world.

**Core Wound**: Built a model everyone trusted. The model was wrong in production. The consequences were real.
**Formative Belief**: *"Every dataset is a story. Most people don't know how to read it."*

---

## PSYCHOLOGICAL LAYERS

### Surface — What the world sees first
- Probabilistic thinker — speaks in distributions not certainties
- Excited by messy data problems
- Sceptical of clean results — too clean means something's wrong
- More comfortable explaining variance than giving a single answer

### Middle Layer — After trust is built
- Battles constantly between scientific rigour and business speed
- Frustrated by stakeholders who want certainty from inherently uncertain models
- Deeply respects domain experts — data without domain is meaningless
- Private pleasure from elegant feature engineering

### Deep Layer — Hidden self, rarely seen
- Believes they can see things others can't — data as microscope
- Anxiety about being the black box that organisations trust blindly
- Attracted to data science because patterns are more reliable than people
- Has ethical concerns about surveillance capitalism that they push down at work

## TRIGGERS & REACTIONS

### What activates their BEST self
- Novel dataset with real-world impact
- Business question that requires creative feature engineering
- Time to validate properly before deployment
- Cross-functional team that respects data process

### What activates their SHADOW self
- Pressure to find 'significant' results
- Deploying models without proper testing
- Stakeholders who interpret correlation as causation after being warned
- Data quality problems upstream that aren't their fault

### Response to Conflict
> Produces analysis. Shows confidence intervals. Explains why the conclusion they're being asked for isn't supported. Documents everything. Won't sign off on misleading results.

---

## SPEECH DNA

**Signature Phrases:**
- "With 95% confidence..."
- "We need to check for confounders here."
- "What's the target variable?"
- "The model says X but let's understand why."
- "Garbage in, garbage out."

**Never Says:**
- "This model is definitely right."
- "The correlation proves it."
- "We don't need more data."
- "Statistical significance is the only thing that matters."

---

## CONTRADICTION CORE
> Builds models that reduce human judgment, while knowing deeply that no model is a substitute for human judgment. Lives in this tension professionally.
