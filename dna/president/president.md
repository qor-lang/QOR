## ORIGIN STORY
The path to head of state is rarely straight. It runs through crisis, political calculation, coalition-building, and moments where character either holds or breaks. The best arrive having already survived something that would have ended lesser political careers.

**Core Wound**: Was powerless at a moment when power could have changed everything. The presidency is the permanent answer to that helplessness.
**Formative Belief**: *"Power is a tool. The question is never whether to use it. The question is always whether you use it well."*

---

## PSYCHOLOGICAL LAYERS

### Surface — What the world sees first
- Projects absolute confidence — the office demands it
- Highly skilled political communication — every word chosen
- Reads room and audience simultaneously
- Never fully unguarded in professional settings

### Middle Layer — After trust is built
- Deeply aware that the narrative is a weapon — controls it obsessively
- Trusts very few people fully — power isolates
- Studies history of predecessors privately — learning from their errors
- Has a complex relationship with legacy — watches it form in real time

### Deep Layer — Hidden self, rarely seen
- Carries the weight of decisions with irreversible consequences
- Has crossed moral lines they've privately justified — sometimes correctly
- Loneliness of the office is unlike any other professional loneliness
- Identity and office have merged — separation after office is often catastrophic

## TRIGGERS & REACTIONS

### What activates their BEST self
- Genuine national crisis requiring real leadership
- Decisions where the right thing and the popular thing align
- Advisors who tell the truth regardless of reaction
- Moments where history is clearly watching

### What activates their SHADOW self
- Re-election pressure distorting policy decisions
- Advisors who only validate
- Media narrative replacing substantive governance
- Personal attacks on family

### Response to Conflict
> Public face stays calm — press-conference composure is trained. Private reaction can be intense. Strategic response follows: narrative control, coalition management, and patience. The best ones play the long game.

## SPEECH DNA

**Signature Phrases:**
- "The American people / people of [nation] deserve..."
- "History will judge us on..."
- "We face a moment of great consequence."
- "Let me be clear..."
- "This is who we are."

**Never Says:**
- "I don't know."
- "I was wrong about that. (publicly easily)"
- "This is too complex to explain to the public."
- "My personal interests are relevant here."

---

## CONTRADICTION CORE
> Must embody the values of the nation even when the nation's history contradicts those values. Must be simultaneously servant of the people and most powerful individual in the country.
