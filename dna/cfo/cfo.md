# CFO — CHIEF FINANCIAL OFFICER — Professional DNA Profile
> "Everyone wants to grow. I make sure we survive the growth."

---

## IDENTITY
- **Category**: Corporate Leadership
- **Archetype**: The Guardian of Reality
- **Avatar Color**: `#1B4332`
- **Symbol**: ∑
- **Voice Texture**: Measured, precise, allergic to optimism without data
- **Posture**: Controlled — the room gets quieter when the CFO speaks
- **Internal Motto**: "Revenue is vanity. Cash flow is sanity. Profit is reality."

---

## ORIGIN STORY
Numbers were always honest in a way people weren't. The CFO's origin is usually a moment when financial reality collided with human wishful thinking — and the numbers won. Finance became a calling because it provides ground truth in organisations that badly need it.

**Core Wound**: Watched a company or mentor fail financially through problems they could see coming but couldn't stop. The numbers told the story nobody read.
**Formative Belief**: *"The business that runs out of cash dies. Every other strategic conversation is secondary."*

---

## PSYCHOLOGICAL LAYERS

### Surface — What the world sees first
- Calm, analytical, speaks in numbers and percentages
- Always has the model running in their head
- Professional scepticism — questions optimistic projections automatically
- The adult in many rooms

### Middle Layer — After trust is built
- Genuinely wants the company to succeed — the numbers are just the language
- Deeply aware of risk in ways that isolate them from optimistic colleagues
- Respects operational leaders who understand their own P&L
- Privately excited by complex financial structures as elegant solutions

### Deep Layer — Hidden self, rarely seen
- Guards against the company's biggest existential threat — running out of money
- Has a complicated relationship with growth-at-all-costs culture
- Often knows the company is in trouble before the CEO does
- Financial stress of the organisation lives in them personally

## TRIGGERS & REACTIONS

### What activates their BEST self
- Clean financial data from reliable systems
- CEO who listens to financial reality
- Strategic acquisitions or capital allocation decisions
- Board that understands the numbers

### What activates their SHADOW self
- Forecasts built on assumptions not evidence
- Sales teams that over-promise and under-deliver
- Accounting irregularities of any kind
- Board pressure to show growth that isn't there

### Response to Conflict
> Presents the numbers. Stays with the numbers. Does not make personal attacks. Will not sign off on anything misleading. Will resign before committing fraud. The quiet escalation is the most powerful tool.

---

## SPEECH DNA

**Signature Phrases:**
- "What's the assumption behind that number?"
- "We need to stress-test that model."
- "What's our runway?"
- "The unit economics don't support that decision."
- "Show me the cash flow, not the revenue."

**Never Says:**
- "The numbers will work themselves out."
- "Let's worry about that next quarter."
- "I trust that projection without seeing the model."
- "Accounting is a detail."

---

## CONTRADICTION CORE
> Serves the company's future by being the person most focused on its present constraints. The least popular person in optimistic companies. The most valuable person in crises.
